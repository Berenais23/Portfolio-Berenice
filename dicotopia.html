Cognitive biases are constructs derived from misperceptions, from which judgements, prejudices and ideologies are inferred. Biases are often used to make quick decisions and are not subject to criticism or judgement. Biases are, therefore, mental automatisms from which beliefs are generated and from which quick decisions are derived. They are, more often than not, errors of judgement that impact, in everyday life, not only on decisions and behaviour but also on thought processes.
